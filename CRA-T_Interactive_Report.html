They are the primary evidence for a targeted 6–9 month refresher cycle.

Shared Regression Profile
What the Three Fragile-Middle Scenarios Have in Common
All three regressions share a structural fingerprint: moderate-difficulty physical diagnostics (not abstract/backend specialist knowledge) that rely on PD-derived vocabulary rather than deep habituated survival anchors (Pprime = 1.0–1.0 at 365d), combined with non-podium physical location (Ccontext = 0.8) and some degree of system alienation (Ese = 0.8). They pass at 30 days because the PD session provides just enough cognitive scaffolding. They fail at 365 days because the Rbase halving from 0.70 to 0.35 removes the floor that was supporting them. None of the 14 original Score 0 scenarios (all specialist-domain failures) regressed — they were already at zero. The regressions came entirely from this fragile-middle tier. A targeted 6–9 month refresher module covering these three diagnostic scenarios — bent pin color-channel mapping, HDMI passive cable length limits, and IR sensor failure sequencing — is the minimum intervention to restore the 72% pass rate and prevent further floor erosion.
Comparative Analysis & Key Findings
What the Two Models Tell Us Together
The comparison between the 30-day PD model and the 365-day full course model surfaces four structural findings that have direct implications for program design, refresher scheduling, and curriculum scope.
Finding A
Score 3 Parity — The Full Course Creates a Durable Ceiling
The 14-week full course at 365 days preserves every Score 3 outcome that the 2-day PD produced at 30 days — identical at 14/50 in both analyses. The REP₃₆₅ model's wider Salign ceiling (2.0 vs 1.2) and higher Pprime ceiling (1.4 vs 1.2) precisely compensate for the halved Rbase (0.35 vs 0.70) in daily-habit scenarios. The scenarios that survive a full year intact share a common profile: they are tied to actions educators perform repeatedly (Salign 2.0), primed by high-stakes survival-failure memories (Pprime 1.4), and executable from visible primary podium controls (Ccontext 1.3). For these 14 scenarios, training depth produces a genuinely decay-proof competency floor — the investment in hands-on simulation (ERP 1.0) pays full dividends at one year. The implication is that curriculum design focused on these three attributes (daily use, survival anchor, podium-accessible fix) can identify which competencies will survive long retention intervals and which require periodic reinforcement.
Finding B
Time Defeats Depth for the Fragile Middle
Three scenarios (3, 25, 26) that held above the 75% REP threshold at 30 days fall below it at 365 days, dropping from the pass column to Score 0. This is the most counterintuitive result of the entire analysis: a significantly more comprehensive 14-week curriculum produces more Score 0 outcomes than the condensed 2-day PD at the 1-year interval. The explanation is not quality of instruction — it is time elapsed and the absence of any reinforcement mechanism. The fragile-middle scenarios share a physical, non-podium diagnostic requirement (Ccontext 0.8) with PD-specific vocabulary (Pprime 1.0) and system alienation from inaccessible hardware (Ese 0.8). The Rbase halving tips these three scenarios over the threshold edge at 1 year. No curriculum redesign prevents this without a dedicated refresher intervention at 6–9 months post-training. The 6% pass rate gap between the PD model and the full course model is explained entirely by these three regressions and the unchanged 14 specialist-domain Score 0 failures.
Finding C
The Specialist Knowledge Floor is Structurally Immovable
All 14 scenarios that scored 0 at 30 days remain at Score 0 at 365 days, without exception. These fall into four specialist-domain curriculum gaps: RF/wireless spectrum management (Scenarios 10, 42), professional audio signal engineering (Scenarios 15, 18, 19, 28, 35, 40), backend hardware protocol/EDID configuration (Scenarios 8, 29, 38, 47), and broadcast/specialist lighting (Scenario 48). These domains are impervious to either training depth or elapsed time because they represent knowledge that is structurally absent from both curricula. Increasing course length to 20 weeks or 30 weeks would not change a single Score 0 outcome unless the specialist modules were added. The REP₃₆₅ formula further penalises these scenarios at 1 year (REP values collapse to 3.6%–5.8% vs 14.7%–42.3% at REP₃₀), but they were already at total abandonment — the penalty applies to an already-failed scenario. The only path from Score 0 for these scenarios is dedicated specialist sub-modules or permanent point-of-action job aids such as laminated classroom infographics.
Finding D
Kit Adequacy Is Time-Invariant — and Structurally Limited
The educator's Proactive AV Tech Kit (spare HDMI cable, USB-C to HDMI dongle, USB-C to USB-A cable, IEC power cord, AA/AAA batteries) produces exactly the same hardware resolution outcomes at 365 days as at 30 days. Kit items either work or they don't — they are not subject to cognitive decay. No kit items address the three regression scenarios (3, 25, 26). Kit expansion with electrical tape, zip ties, and a USB-A to USB-B cable would upgrade 5 scenarios (7, 14, 23, 31, 39) from Score 2 or Score 1 to Score 3 at both time intervals — a 10% jump in Full Resolution rate for approximately $6 in additional items. Critically, no kit expansion of any kind addresses the 14 specialist-domain Score 0 scenarios. The Score 0 floor is exclusively a curriculum problem, not a kit problem. This distinction is foundational to remediation planning: the two levers are independent and address different failure classes.

Primary Conclusion
Course Depth Cannot Substitute for Refresher Timing
The full 14-week college course at 365 days matches the condensed 2-day professional development at 30 days on Score 3 outcomes (14 each), but the 1-year decay window results in Score 0 regressions for 3 scenarios that had been mitigated under the PD model and lowers the overall threshold pass rate from 72% to 66%, confirming that course depth alone cannot substitute for a 6–9 month refresher cycle on the fragile-middle diagnostic scenarios. The recommended intervention sequence is: (1) Add the four targeted curriculum modules (~40–50 min) to eliminate all 14 specialist-domain Score 0 failures; (2) Add electrical tape, USB-A to USB-B cable, and zip ties to the Proactive AV Tech Kit (~$6) for 5 additional Score 3 outcomes; (3) Implement a 6–9 month refresher module specifically targeting Scenarios 3, 25, and 26 to prevent the fragile-middle regression at the 1-year mark and restore the 72% pass rate floor.
