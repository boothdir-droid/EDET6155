The Disruptive Reality

In modern education, physical classroom technology is essential. However, when foundational audiovisual (AV) hardware fails, the instructional flow stops. This isn't just an inconvenience; it's a critical loss of learning time.

Existing research highlights the macro-impact: minor classroom interruptions accumulate to a staggering 10 to 20 days of lost instructional time per academic year (Kraft & Monti-Nussbaum, 2021). Our project addresses the localized, mechanical failures driving this loss.

Multi-Platform AI Anti-Bias Framework

To ensure scientific integrity and eliminate algorithmic bias, data generation, course development, and analysis are intentionally decoupled across three independent AI architectures.

1

Gemini Architecture

Data Generation & Containment

Compiles and locks the raw dataset of 50 hardware failure scenarios. Isolated from course materials.

2

Copilot Architecture

Curriculum & Agent Design

Develops the "AV Foundations" curriculum and maps troubleshooting logic for the real-time agent.

3

Claude Architecture

Evaluative Analysis

Acts as a blind third-party to cross-analyze the dataset against the curriculum to calculate mitigation likelihood.

The Dataset: 50 Real-World Scenarios

The core of this research is a pristine dataset of 50 distinct hardware failure scenarios. Crucially, this dataset strictly excludes software errors, focusing entirely on physical hardware and local device output configurations.

The Remediation Strategy

✓

Phase 1: Training Course

"AV Foundations and Troubleshooting for Teachers"

A structured curriculum designed to equip educators with core mechanical competencies, cable identification skills, and systematic physical troubleshooting logic before failures occur.

  • Cable & Connector Identification
  • Basic Signal Flow Understanding
  • Output Resolution Configuration
⚙

Phase 2: Real-Time Tooling

Interactive AI-Driven Q&A Agent

In a live classroom disruption, a teacher inputs immediate hardware symptoms. The agent instantly outputs a prioritized, context-aware sequence of physical troubleshooting steps to minimize downtime.

Teacher: "Projector says 'Unsupported Format'."
AI Agent: "Check laptop display settings. Change output from 4K to 1080p."

Ultimate Goal: The Mitigation Likelihood Score

The final analytical phase will process the 50 scenarios against these remediation tools to generate a Mitigation Likelihood Score, proving whether targeted training and AI support effectively protect instructional time.
