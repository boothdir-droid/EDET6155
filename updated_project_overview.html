1. Genesis: The Problem Statement & Literature Review

The project originated from a critical gap identified between modern instructional dependence on digital tools and a total lack of hardware troubleshooting literacy in basic teacher preparation programs.

  • The Core Challenge: Routine classroom presentation technology failures—such as physical cable disconnections, incorrect display resolutions, mismatched aspect ratios, and failed digital handshakes—permanently drain up to 9% of planned learning hours. This translates to over 200 hours of academic downtime per school annually[cite: 5, 10].
  • Systemic Strain: Post-pandemic teacher fatigue, mounting educational workloads, and understaffed institutional IT support networks leave teachers unsupported during critical, real-time classroom errors[cite: 5, 10]. The resulting friction causes an acute lack of environmental trust, pushing weary educators to abandon digital learning assets entirely[cite: 5, 10].
  • The Administrative Gap: Current intervention methodologies rely almost exclusively on lagging, reactive software workshops or slow IT helpdesk ticketing mechanisms[cite: 5, 10]. These do not teach an instructor how to visually evaluate or physically resolve local signal routing and connectivity faults[cite: 5, 10].

2. The Core Architecture: The 3-Silo Sandbox Model

To systematically evaluate and address this challenge under the ADDIE instructional design framework without disrupting active classrooms or demanding live human subjects, a multi-platform, decoupled AI sandbox loop was engineered[cite: 5, 10]. This environment was constructed across three specific operational silos to isolate data generation from evaluation, completely removing developer bias[cite: 5, 10]:

Silo 1: The Architect

Platform: Microsoft Copilot

Collaborates as a structural learning experience partner to build out the foundational hardware-focused professional development curriculum, maps technical terminology, and shapes core instructional content loops[cite: 5, 10].

Silo 2: The Environment

Platform: Google Gemini

Acts as the unpredictable real-world classroom factor[cite: 5, 10]. It synthesizes web-scraped problem logs to generate a locked dataset of 50 localized physical technology failure scenarios stratified across 5 grade bands[cite: 5, 10].

Silo 3: The Evaluator

Platform: Anthropic Claude

Acts as an independent third-party auditor[cite: 5, 10]. It algorithmically processes each classroom failure against the curriculum's structural rules to accurately predict time-loss mitigation and continuous learning preservation[cite: 2, 5].

3. Critical Scope Changes & Subsequent Research Pivot

The implementation path underwent a significant architectural pivot following intermediate peer reviews and deep cognitive science investigations[cite: 3, 5]:

Original Concept

The Dual-Pronged Intervention

The baseline design proposed a two-part intervention: a targeted technical curriculum to train educators on hardware properties, backed at run-time by an interactive, conversational AI Troubleshooting Agent using a highly restricted RAG framework to advise teachers during active faults[cite: 5].

Scope Reduction Pivot

Deselection of the AI Troubleshooting Agent

To establish maximum feasibility and isolate pure human capability, the AI Troubleshooting Agent was completely removed from the project scope[cite: 1, 5]. The project pivoted to examine exactly how effective localized physical hardware training is on its own, stripping away any active AI support nets or real-time conversational assistance[cite: 5].

Scientific Overhaul

Integration of the $REP_{30}$ Mathematical Filter

Rather than relying on flat, unscientific 50% knowledge drop-off assumptions, subsequent research into adult cognitive architecture was integrated via the 30-Day Summer PD Retention and Learning Transfer Guide[cite: 3, 5]. Human memory decay under high-stress classroom environments is now calculated using a non-linear seven-variable formula[cite: 3]:

REP30 = (Rbase × Salign × Ccomp × Ese × Pprime) × Ccontext × ERP

This allows variables like Schema Alignment ($S_{align}$), Context-Dependent Reinstatement ($C_{context}$), and Expertise Reversal Penalties ($ERP$) to be assessed uniquely for each individual scenario, capturing nuanced human capability rather than static course summaries[cite: 3].

4. Finalized Project Deliverables & Output Blueprint

The final completed project settles into a rigorous, automated testing framework built to establish a predictive data baseline before future human deployments[cite: 5]. It consists of the following four primary pillars:

  1. The Professional Development Curriculum Matrix: A targeted, hardware-focused learning framework covering physical cable validation, input signal flow paths, and local device configuration panels, designed to support a specialized SUNY CPD digital badge[cite: 5, 10].
  2. The Locked Environmental Dataset: 50 highly localized, grade-band stratified failure scenarios (covering Kindergarten up to Higher Education) testing physical continuity and configuration flaws under high pressure[cite: 5, 10].
  3. The Individualized Scenario Evaluation Filter: An automated parsing protocol inside Claude Projects that maps each scenario step-by-step against localized toolkit spares (HDMI, adapter dongles, power cables) and unique $REP_{30}$ variables, requiring a calculated threshold of ≥75% memory retention to pass[cite: 1, 3].
  4. The CRA-T Diagnostic Synthesis Report: A standardized, objective data output that scores each post-intervention scenario on a 4-point instructional continuity rubric (Scores 0-3), providing explicit engineering justifications and identifying clear curriculum flaws or optimization zones[cite: 1, 3].
