The Iterative Process: Grounding Predictable Retention

Reevaluating Cognitive Filtration Models in Technical Training Research

Methodological Paradigm Shift

Project Purpose Only: This log documents an evolving research workflow designed to analyze classroom instructional decay. Rather than relying on static assumptions, this iterative loop leverages prompt generation, deep domain literature analysis, and AI analysis to build a verified baseline for technical learning loss.

1. Overcoming the "Guesswork" Baseline

Early exploratory models of our cognitive filter relied primarily on estimated values, assumptions, and generalized assertions regarding how effectively an educator retains troubleshooting information after a training session. While useful for constructing basic conceptual wireframes, utilizing numbers that can at best be described as structured guesses introduces a high margin of error when mapping out practical mitigation windows for instructional tech disruptions.

To address this vulnerability, our research transitioned into an iterative, multi-tiered pipeline of targeted prompt generation, external analytical verification, and programmatic data synthesis. By grounding our variables in established cognitive load frameworks and documented retention curves, we replace arbitrary data points with defensible, contextual parameters.

2. Comparative Evolution of the Research Model

Analytical Dimension Original Conceptual Plan Old Iterative AI Analysis Framework New
Variable Acquisition Relied on arbitrary percentage curves and generic assumptions about technical skill decay. Extracted through specialized programmatic prompting backed by behavioral learning theories.
Cognitive Friction Mapping Treated as a static penalty value across all types of hardware failures. Dynamically weights variable cognitive load against the specific complexity of the troubleshooting task.
Predictable Retention Estimated through standard linear decay intervals without environmental context. Calculates interactive mitigation factors, isolating targeted training vs. raw technical knowledge dropoff.
